Jane Austen seems to have had keen insight into the behavior of the people of her time, but also into the "algebra" of human behavior - the structures and actions that govern human behavior - and a remarkable ability to translate this algebra to the page in such a way that her observations feel sharp and incisive over two hundred years later.
